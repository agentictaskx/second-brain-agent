# Email Status Brief

## When to Use

When emailing a status update or brief to VPs, GMs, or other executives. The tone is confident and data-backed. Every sentence must earn its place.

## Structure

1. **Subject line** — concise, informative, includes status signal if applicable
2. **BLUF** — 1-2 sentences with the bottom line
3. **Supporting points** — 2-3 bolded thesis statements with inline metrics
4. **Ask or next step** — what you need from the reader (if anything)

For longer status emails (weekly rollups sent to leadership):

1. **Subject line**
2. **BLUF** — 2-3 sentences
3. **Status table** — RAG per workstream/squad
4. **Key highlights** — 3-5 bullets with metrics
5. **Key risks** — 2-3 bullets with explicit asks
6. **Next steps** — who does what by when

## Template

### Short Status Email
```
Subject: [Workstream] — [Status Signal] — [Key Point]

[BLUF: 1-2 sentences. What's the state? What do you need?]

**[Thesis 1]** — [supporting detail with metric].
**[Thesis 2]** — [supporting detail with metric].
**[Thesis 3 / Ask]** — [what you need from the reader, by when].
```

### Weekly Status Email
```
Subject: [Workstream] Weekly Status — [Date] — [Overall RAG]

[BLUF: 2-3 sentences. What moved the needle? What's the top risk?]

| Squad / Area | Status | Key Delta |
|-------------|--------|-----------|
| [Squad 1] | [Green/Yellow/Red] | [1-line change from last week] |
| [Squad 2] | [Green/Yellow/Red] | [1-line change from last week] |

**Highlights:**
- [Achievement with metric] ([Squad])
- [Achievement with metric] ([Squad])
- [Achievement with metric]

**Risks:**
- [Risk] — need [what] from [who] by [when]
- [Risk] — [status and mitigation]

**Next Steps:**
- [Action] — [owner] — [date]
- [Action] — [owner] — [date]
```

## Tone Rules

- **Confident.** No hedging. "We need X" not "We might consider X."
- **Data-backed.** Metrics inline, not in separate tables. "5M users processed in <2h" not "significant improvement."
- **Concise.** 10 sentences max for a status email. If it's longer, it's a document, not an email.
- **Professional but not stiff.** Medium formality. No "Dear" or "Best regards."
- **Bold thesis statements a VP could quote.** Make it easy for them to forward your email up.

## Subject Line Patterns

| Type | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Status update | `[Workstream] — [RAG] — [key point]` | `User Understanding — Green — Denoising runtime 5M users in <2h` |
| Weekly rollup | `[Workstream] Weekly — [Date] — [RAG]` | `User Understanding Weekly — Apr 9 — Green` |
| Escalation | `[Workstream] — [Red/Blocked] — [blocker]` | `User Understanding — Blocked — DoCA convergence needs LT decision` |
| FYI | `FYI: [topic]` | `FYI: V3 Dev moved Green → Amber this week` |

## Examples

### Good BLUF
> User Understanding is Green overall. Denoising runtime hit the 5M-user milestone (processing in <2h, down from 12h/day) and MT/Spark capacity is validated for 1M users. Top risk: DoCA convergence path needs Vikas's response on UMS mapping — 13 days and counting.

### Bad BLUF
> Hi Julia, hope you're doing well! I wanted to send you an update on how things are going with the User Understanding workstream. We've been making good progress across both squads and there are some exciting developments to share.

### Good Thesis Statement
> **Denoising runtime is production-ready at 5M scale** — 170M activities processed in <2h, a 6x improvement from the continuous 12h/day pipeline.

### Bad Thesis Statement
> We've been working on improving the denoising runtime and it's getting better.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Don't start with "Hope this finds you well."** BLUF, always.
- **Don't hedge.** "We believe," "We might consider" — cut these.
- **Don't use apologetic framing.** "Sorry for the extra email" — if it's worth sending, don't apologize.
- **Don't bury the ask.** If you need something from the reader, put it in the BLUF or the first two lines.
- **Don't send a wall of text.** 10 sentences max. Link to a document for details.
- **Don't use separate attachments for data.** Inline the key metrics. VPs don't open attachments.
